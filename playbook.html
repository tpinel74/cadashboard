Purpose of This Document

This document describes how the CA Membership Acquisition (Marketing Department) team operates — how it allocates resources, structures its work, builds monthly plans, and holds itself accountable to results.

2
Team Objective

The Membership Acquisition team exists to generate membership demand — qualified leads, online conversions, and full-funnel engagement — and to build the capabilities that make that demand engine stronger over time.

Immediate Delivery
Lead Pipeline
Generating the lead pipeline that the Sales team depends on each month to meet organizational membership goals.
Long-Term Investment
Capability Building
Investing in the channels, systems, and practices that make future delivery more effective and less expensive.
Core principle
Both parts of this objective must be held in balance. The operating model described here is designed to protect both — not optimize for one at the expense of the other.
3
Resource Allocation Framework

The team allocates its collective effort — focus, attention, and time — across two primary lanes in a deliberate and protected ratio.

75% Execution
25% Build
Current Execution — Cat 0 + Cat 1
Capability Building — Cat 2

75% of the team's collective effort is directed toward current execution — running the campaigns, channels, and tactics that generate leads and support membership sales in the present month.

25% of the team's collective effort is protected for capability-building projects that improve the long-term performance of the acquisition engine. These projects are not expected to generate leads in the current month. They build the machine that generates more leads in future months.

Important distinction
This split reflects effort and focus — not budget. With small exceptions, Marketing Acquisition spend remains directed toward paid acquisition channels. Budget and resource support for capability projects are considered independently for each project.

Note that this ratio is a guide, not a rigid timesheet requirement. Team members will be guided by internal team workplans and oversight provided by the division manager.

Why this balance matters: Without the 75% execution commitment, the organization fails to meet its membership targets today. Without the 25% capability investment, the team's ability to meet those targets erodes over time as market conditions change, new competitors emerge, and existing channels become less efficient.

The 25% lane also protects the team from an accumulation of underdeveloped ideas and partially implemented projects. By maintaining a managed backlog and limiting active capability projects to one or two at a time, the team can build new capabilities thoroughly rather than spreading effort across too many initiatives simultaneously.

5
Cat 0 — Current Execution
Cat 0Part of the 75% execution lane

Cat 0 is the existing execution layer. It represents the channels, campaigns, and tactics that have demonstrated consistent lead generation performance.

Cat 0 is organized across three campaign "streams" that operate in parallel throughout the year. Each stream has a distinct strategic purpose. Together they represent the full range of marketing activities the team deploys in pursuit of lead generation and membership growth across the full customer journey and nurturing funnel.

Each stream contains two types of activity: always-on tactics that run continuously regardless of the month, and active campaigns that are time-bound activations deployed in service of specific seasonal or promotional objectives.

6
The Three Streams
Stream 1
Direct Acquisition
Generate marketing-sourced leads and drive membership conversions through demand capture, paid social, CRM cold lead reactivation, and member referral activation. This stream carries the primary lead volume and clearest attribution information.
Stream 2
Organic Growth
Build sustainable earned lead generation through search visibility, website content, and organic discoverability. This stream grows over time as content and SEO investment compounds.
Stream 3
Brand Storytelling
Build awareness and consideration among community members who are not yet in the marketing ecosystem. This stream feeds the top of the funnel that Streams 1 and 2 convert.
7
Cat 1 — Improvement Initiatives
Cat 1Part of the 75% execution lane

Cat 1 is the improvement layer within the 75% execution allocation. It represents focused experiments, analyses, and optimizations within the existing marketing system — designed to make the current machine perform better without adding new channels or capabilities.

Examples of Cat 1 work
A/B testing a campaign creative or CTA  ·  Analyzing audience segment performance within an existing channel  ·  Testing a new landing page structure  ·  Running a cohort analysis on lapsed leads  ·  Optimizing the timing or sequencing of a nurture workflow

Cat 1 initiatives are scoped, time-boxed, and measured against a defined hypothesis. They have a start, a period of observation, and a conclusion that either gets implemented into Cat 0 or informs a future iteration. The team maintains a backlog of Cat 1 candidates and typically runs one or two at a time to ensure meaningful conclusions can be drawn.

Cat 1 work sits within the 75% allocation — it does not add to it. Running a Cat 1 initiative means making a deliberate choice about where within the execution lane to invest improvement effort.

8
Cat 2 — Capability Building
Cat 2The 25% build lane

The 25% capability lane is reserved for Cat 2 projects: strategic investments that add new features to the marketing system. New channels, new platforms, new data infrastructure, new partner programs — things that do not yet exist in the current execution layer and that require deliberate building before they can perform.

Cat 2 projects are distinct from Cat 1 in that they are building something net new rather than improving something that already exists. They require a defined project scope, a clear rationale, and explicit completion conditions.

Standard project documentation (required before entering the active lane)
One-line objective — what is this for and what problem does it solve
Completion conditions — how will we know when this project has finished its time in the Cat 2 lane
Dependencies — what does this project depend on, and what depends on it
Resourcing — estimated effort required from the team
Performance signals — what will we watch during the Cat 2 phase to understand whether it is on track
9
Graduation & Backlog

Graduation occurs when a Cat 2 project meets its defined conditions — typically a combination of operational readiness and demonstrated sustainability within the team's existing capacity. Graduation means the project moves into the Cat 0/1 execution layer as an ongoing channel or tactic, freeing the Cat 2 lane for the next backlog project.

If a project does not meet its graduation conditions, it either extends or is closed out. Closing a project is not a failure — it is the system working correctly. A closed Cat 2 project means the team learned something and can redirect that capacity toward a more promising investment.

The backlog is a managed list of capability-building candidates that have been identified and prioritized. Projects do not enter the active Cat 2 lane directly from an idea — they move through the backlog as prior projects graduate or close.

Active project limit
Typically, no more than two Cat 2 projects are active at any time. More than two active projects dilutes focus, slows progress, and reduces the likelihood that any single project is built to the standard required for successful graduation.

The backlog is owned jointly by the team lead and the Director of Marketing. The team lead manages the sequencing and implementation of active projects. The Director retains influence over prioritization — particularly for projects with budget implications, cross-team dependencies, or organizational visibility requirements.

10
Accountability & Measurement

The team is held accountable to two primary outcomes that apply to the 75% execution lane.

Primary Outcome
Lead Volume
The number of marketing-sourced leads generated in a given month. This is the primary volume target and takes priority over cost efficiency. The monthly target is set in relation to the organizational sales goal using an 80% marketing attribution ratio.
Lead volume > cost efficiency
Efficiency Signal
Aggregate Cost Per Lead
The blended cost per lead across all marketing-sourced leads — paid, organic, CRM-activated, and referral. This is an efficiency signal, not a ceiling that should cause the team to sacrifice lead volume. A target CPL and a review threshold are set each month.
Signal, not a sacrifice trigger
Self-regulating control mechanism
These two outcomes function as a system. If the team invests too heavily in Streams 2 or 3 at the expense of Stream 1, lead volume will signal it quickly. If the team over-relies on expensive paid channels without building organic and referral sources, CPL will signal it.

Five-Layer Promotional Framework

The team operates a fixed, five-layer promotional framework that provides structure and predictability for both the acquisition team and the sales team. This framework establishes a set of standing promotional mechanisms that activate based on defined conditions.

Monthly plans describe which layers are active and how they are being deployed — they do not redesign the framework.

Layer 1
Always On
Permanent
Description
The standing membership value proposition — what CA offers and why it is worth joining — available at all times without expiration pressure.
Activation
Runs continuously — no trigger required.
Audience
All prospects
Layer 2
Full-Month Offer
Monthly
Description
A monthly incentive tied to a specific membership type or commitment level. Creates meaningful value without deep discounting. Changes seasonally based on strategic context.
Activation
Set at the start of each month — runs the full month.
Audience
Non-members in consideration
Layer 3
Referral Offer
Standing
Description
A standing incentive for existing members who refer new members. Runs concurrently with the monthly offer. Activates the advocacy-to-acquisition funnel.
Activation
Permanent within defined referral windows — may be enhanced seasonally.
Audience
Existing members
Layer 4
Flash Sale
Mid-Month
Description
A short-duration, high-urgency offer creating a conversion spike mid-month. Typically 24–48 hours. Limited quantity or time-bound to drive immediacy.
Activation
Mid-month, typically around day 14–15.
Audience
Active leads and warm prospects in pipeline
Layer 5
Surge Offer
Conditional
Description
A pace-triggered contingency offer that activates only if the team is tracking significantly below target at a defined mid-month checkpoint. Not deployed unless the trigger condition is met.
Activation
Triggered by pace — activates only when needed.
Audience
Broadest available audience
Framework design intent
The five-layer framework is designed to work as a system. The always-on layer provides a permanent foundation. The full-month offer creates a seasonal context and a reason to act. The referral layer generates lower-cost leads from the existing member base. The flash sale creates urgency within the month. The surge offer provides a managed response to underperformance without requiring an ad hoc decision in the moment.
13
Monthly Planning Process

Each month's marketing plan is built against this operating model. The monthly plan is a distinct document — not a section of this one.

What a monthly plan contains
Monthly business objectives (lead volume & CPL targets)  ·  Monthly strategic themes (seasonal context, messaging hierarchy, offer architecture)  ·  Current execution layer — Cat 0 streams with always-on and active campaigns  ·  Active Cat 1 improvement initiatives  ·  Active Cat 2 capability projects
What a monthly plan does NOT contain
Internal workplan details — who does what, task assignments  ·  Channel-level tactical specifications — keywords, audience parameters, creative specs, platform settings. These live in channel-level operating documents.
