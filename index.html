UTM Source / Medium Taxonomy
Why this exists

Every lead entering our system through a marketing channel carries a Source and Medium pair — together they're the foundation of all lead reporting and channel analysis. Source identifies the originating platform (where the lead came from); Medium identifies the mechanism within that channel (how they got here).

Campaign, Content, and Keyword add further dimensionality — which initiative, which specific asset/variant, and (for paid search) which search term — but Source and Medium are required on every tagged link and lead record.

This table is the canonical reference. New combinations are added deliberately as new channels are activated; leads with malformed or unrecognized values get corrected at the source.

Usage Notes
  • Google campaign types (Search, Display, Demand Gen, Performance Max) are distinguished via the Campaign field naming convention, not via separate Medium values — ensure campaign names consistently include the type.
  • crm as a source reflects the CRM as a marketing investment channel, analogous to "google / paid." It's applied when a CRM-driven communication (email, SMS, or app announcement) produces a conversion event — not at original lead capture.
  • All offline physical materials (signage, flyers, direct mail, collateral) use a QR code as the lead capture mechanism, attributed as Source=offline. "offline / qr" covers most signage and poster-based generation; "offline / mail" is reserved for direct mail.
  • "bing / paid", "tiktok / paid", "reddit / social", "reddit / paid", and "ai / paid" aren't yet active but are expected — they'll be added when those channels go live.
  • prospect_wizard will be deprecated when SalesIQ goes live; leads will flow into their true source buckets with upstream UTM parameters passed correctly.
